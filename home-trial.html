Did You

Know?

Did you know that Spain is the second most visited country in the world? Its diverse attractions range from the architectural marvels of Antoni Gaudí in Barcelona to the ancient wonders of the Alhambra in Granada. Spain is also home to the world's oldest restaurant, Botín, which has been serving patrons since 1725. With over 3,000 fiestas celebrated each year, including the famous La Tomatina festival, Spain is a land of lively traditions and festivities. From the bustling streets of Madrid to the serene beaches of the Balearic Islands, Spain offers an endless array of experiences waiting to be explored. Join us as we unravel the fascinating tapestry of Spain's history, culture, and natural beauty.

Find More About Your Destination

Beyond its urban attractions, Barcelona offers beautiful beaches along its coastline, providing the perfect escape for sunbathing, swimming, and water sports.

Madrid is a dynamic and diverse city known for its history, culture, gastronomy, and vibrant atmosphere. It attracts millions of visitors each year who come to experience its rich cultural heritage and cosmopolitan lifestyle.

Seville, often spelled Sevilla in Spanish, is a historic and vibrant city located in the southern region of Spain, known as Andalusia.It experiences long hours of sunlight throughout the year, making it an attractive destination for visitors seeking warm weather.

Marbella boasts over 27 kilometers of coastline with beautiful sandy beaches, including the popular Playa de la Fontanilla, Playa de Puerto Banús, and Playa de San Pedro Alcántara. The beaches offer a wide range of water sports activities and are lined with beach clubs and restaurants.
